# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Primary user is the owner or small operations team at Heather Benjamin Jewelry. They are not expected to be technical. They use Artisan while handling wholesale orders, production notes, Bali team handoffs, packing checks, and customer updates.

Context: tired, busy, switching between PDFs, spreadsheets, emails, DMs, product catalog memory, and production decisions. The product must reduce thinking load, not add another dashboard to manage.

## Product Purpose

Artisan turns messy purchase orders into a guided operational workflow:

1. Add order.
2. Check what Artisan found.
3. Answer only the missing or uncertain questions.
4. Create production sheet.
5. Create packing checklist.
6. Draft customer update.

Success means a judge or owner understands the workflow in under 3 minutes, sees unresolved data blocked before production, and trusts that the app will not invent production facts.

## Brand Personality

Calm, tactile, precise.

Voice: plain-spoken assistant. No AI hype, no technical jargon, no dashboard theater. The product should feel like a careful operations helper with good taste.

## Anti-references

- Claude-style AI cowork dashboard as primary UI.
- Developer console, prompt editor, tool-call log, or token/status cockpit.
- Dense analytics dashboard with fake metrics.
- Raw extraction JSON or database-shaped tables as first experience.
- Generic SaaS purple gradients, glass cards, or noisy AI decorations.
- Luxury storefront replica. This is an internal operations tool, not a shop page.

## Design Principles

1. One decision at a time.
2. AI drafts, human approves.
3. Hide complexity until needed.
4. Brand warmth through materials, typography, and calm spacing.
5. Production blockers must be obvious and impossible to miss.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA for core flows. Body text and form labels must be high contrast. Motion must be optional and respect reduced-motion preferences. The guided tour must never block the main workflow permanently; users need Skip and replay controls.
